My Background

I have always held a strong passion and curiosity for technology. It started with tinkering with electronics and evolved to basic HTML for personal websites like Xanga and Myspace. Eventually, it would progress to hardware repair and software development. In high school, I considered pursuing a career in Computer Science. Still, I opted to pursue a career in music education instead because of my high school choir director's impact on my life. Throughout college, I stayed connected to technology in many ways such as working for my University's IT department as well as starting a small business repairing software and hardware for small businesses.

After college, however, it seemed like no matter the industry I found myself in, I gravitated towards optimizing aspects of my job performance using various technologies. During my time in the water utility industry, I implemented many of Google's maps/places features to reduce friction in locating water meters and valves. My work caught the attention of the CEO, who eventually tasked me with developing a new department for the company; an electronic meter reading department. I served as a liaison to a contracting software developer. Assisting in the UX/UI design and finding/reporting bugs. It then became my responsibility to recruit and train new employees on the software, and within a matter of weeks our four-person department increased the efficiency of our company's meter reading tenfold. In the education field, to maximize the efficiency and safety of our student dismissal system I designed and implemented a basic solution integrating with Google Docs and Google Sheets that that linked multiple teachers and stations with student information and streamlined how car riders were queued and accounted for during the dismissal phases. I enjoyed seeing a prototype turn into a basic system and then iterated upon with UI updates to further enhance the usability for everyone involved. As a result of my continual work in technology, the district's lead technology coordinator recruited me to lead a technology seminar for other employees.

After nearly a decade of successful work in multiple industries, I began learning programming languages. In my last years of teaching, I started learning Javascript through an app called Grass Hopper before eventually deciding to immerse myself at App Academy, a 10,000+ hour coding boot camp.

Nearly four years after leaving education and fully immersing myself in programming, I have developed a Full Stack application used by the US Navy and assisted in developing a social exploration app using VR technologies.
