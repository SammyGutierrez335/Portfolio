My Background

I have always held a strong passion and curiosity for technology. It started with tinkering with electronics and evolved to basic html for personal websites like Xanga and Myspace. Eventually it would progress to hardware repair and software development. In high school, I considered pursuing a career in Computer Science but opted to pursuit a career in music education instead because of the impact my high school choir director made on my life. Throughout college, I stayed connected to technology in many ways such as working for my University's IT department as well as starting a small business repairing software and hardware for small businesses.

After college however, it seemed liked no matter the industry I found myself in, I gravitated towards optimizing aspects of my job performance using various technologies. During my time in the water utility industry I implemented much of Google's my maps/places software to reduce friction in locating water meters and valves. My work caught the attention of the CEO who eventually tasked me with the goal of developing a completely electronic meter reading department. I served as a liaison to a contracting software developer. Assisting in the UX/UI design as well as finding/reporting bugs. It then became my responsibility to recruit and train new employees on the software and within a matter of weeks our four-person department had increased the efficiency of our companies meter reading tenfold. In the education field, In an effort to maximize efficiency and the safety of our student dismissal system I designed and implemented a basic solution integrating with Google Docs and Google Sheets that that linked multiple teachers and stations with student information and streamlined how car riders were queued and accounted for during the dismissal phases. I enjoyed seeing a prototype turn into a basic system and then iterated upon with UI updates to further enhance the usability for everyone involved. As a result of my continual work in technology, the district's lead technology coordinator recruited me to lead a technology seminar for other employees.

It was after nearly a decade of successful work in multiple industries that I began learning programming languages. In my last years teaching, I started teaching myself Javascript through app called Grass Hopper before eventually deciding to immerse myself at App Academy, a 10,000+ hour coding bootcamp.

Nearly, four years after leaving education and fully immersing myself in programming I have developed Full Stack application used by the US Navy as well as assisted in the development of a social exploration app using VR technologies.
