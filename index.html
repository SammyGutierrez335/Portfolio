My Background

Since Jr High School, I have had a strong passion and curiousity for technology. It started with basic html editing on sites like Xanga and Myspace but later developed into software and hardware repair. I considered pursuing a career in Computer Science but opted to pursuit a career in music education instead because of the impact my high school choir director made on my life. Throughout college I stayed connected to technology in many ways, such as working for my University's IT department as well as starting a business repairing software and hardware for small businesses.

After college, it seemed liked no matter industry I found myself in, I gravitated towards optimize my job using technology. A few examples include developing and managing a electronic meter reading department at a water utility operations company, also serving as a liason to a software developer providing UX/UI design enhancements as well as finding/reporting bugs in the software. Lastly, in the education field, I would constantly be assisting other teachers in ways they could implement technology in their classrooms. I even was hired by the district to run a technology summer seminar for other district employees. I utilized google docs/sheets and conditional logic to create a dismissal procedure that linked multiple teachers and stations with student information that allowed the school to stream line how students were being dismissed.

It was after nearly a decade of successful work in multiple industries that made me realize my passion for programming. I truly enjoyed strategizing solutions, debugging, and creating a innovative products. In my last year in education, I used the programming app Grass Hopper to teach myself javascript.

Almost a year after leaving education and fully immersing myself in programming I working as a Front End Developer and each new day.
